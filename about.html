About me

Hello! I'm Jaime, I'm 22 years old, and I'm a software developer. After completing my studies in Multiplatform Application Development (DAM), I have been working in the industry for a year now. During this time, I’ve been able to apply everything I learned in the classroom to real projects, which has been an incredible experience. In this blog, I want to share my journey, from my beginnings as a student to the challenges and achievements I’ve experienced in my first year as a professional.

About the Blog

In this space, I will focus on topics that I am passionate about and that I have learned throughout my career as a developer. I will share knowledge about programming, from basic concepts to solutions for complex problems I’ve encountered in my work. I will discuss languages like Java, Kotlin, and other technologies I use in multplatform application development. You will also find articles on best practices, productivity tools for programmers, tips for optimizing your code, and how to stay updated in a rapidly evolving technological world. If you’re interested in improving your skills or learning more about the world of development, this is the place for you.

Skills and Experience

Throughout my training in DAM and my first year as a professional developer, I have acquired a range of skills and experiences that have allowed me to grow in the world of software development. I specialize in languages like Java and Kotlin, although I have also worked with SQL and some web technologies. Multiplatform application development is my primary focus, where I have learned to create robust solutions for both Android and other systems. In addition to technical skills, I have had the opportunity to participate in real projects that have taught me how to work in teams, manage deadlines, and solve problems under pressure. From developing applications from scratch to improving existing code, each project has been a challenge that has helped me evolve as a programmer. I am constantly exploring new tools and methodologies, such as using Git for version control and implementing best programming practices to ensure clean and maintainable code.
